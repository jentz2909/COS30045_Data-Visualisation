The tourism industry in Sarawak plays a pivotal role in shaping the economic landscape of this enchanting Malaysian state. Nestled on the island of Borneo, often referred to as the "Land of Hornbills," Sarawak stands out as the third-largest island in the world, located just north of the equator. This captivating region boasts azure skies, tropical breezes, and an abundance of natural beauty, offering visitors the opportunity to bask in sandy beaches and palm-fringed landscapes.

Sarawak is not only Malaysia's largest state but is also home to a rich tapestry of 27 distinct ethnic groups, each with its own unique language, culture, and way of life. Its equatorial climate, lush tropical rainforests, and teeming biodiversity make it a haven for adventure seekers. Sarawak encompasses diverse natural wonders, including the meandering Rajang River, the longest river in Malaysia; the impressive Bakun Dam, one of the largest dams in Southeast Asia; and the historic Niah Cave, one of the earliest human settlements in the region.

In this assignment, we delve deeper into Sarawak's tourism economy, where data visualization will be employed to illustrate the dynamics of domestic and foreign tourist arrivals to Sarawak.
